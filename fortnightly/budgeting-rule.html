Self-reliance Basic of Finances Budgeting Rule

The Essentials, Wants, and Savings Budgeting (EWS ) Rule comes based on the popularized 50/30/20 Budget. However, the 50/30/20 Budget can be unfeasible for those with low incomes living in areas with a high cost of living. For example, housing, utilities and health care costs would increase by over 50% of their income. The EWS Rule allows you to move the percentage of your expenses according to your necessities, for example, a 70/10/20 Budget.

The EWS Budgeting Rule

The 50/30/20 Budget helps you to divide your after-tax income (earnings) among essentials, wants, and savings. The EWS Budget is flexible since you can adjust the EWS percentage according to your income and necessities. A balanced budget can help you determine how much you should spend and on what things.

Essentials

Essentials are things you certainly have to pay for, or you cannot live without these kinds of stuff lightly. Essentials include things like rent, groceries, transportation, and utilities.

Wants

Wants are stuff you desire but do not need that all to survive. These include vacations, dining out, streaming services, and hot drinks.

Savings

Savings include retirement contributions, investments and debt payments.

How does it work?

First, know your monthly income. Second, know how much you receive in your bank account. Third, know how much you spend, classify your expenses by categories and check up on what you are overspending. Fourth, plan your budget according to the EWS rule to avoid debt and overspending. Finally, follow your budget plan.

Overview

Purpose

Develop a web site for a fortnightly budget and expenses tracker. I want to help people with their personal finances budget and expenses applying the gospel principles for self-reliance and the 50/30/20 budget rule. The web site will help people becoming a wise and faithful steward under a unified approach to finances. This site will help to remind paying tithes and offerings. This web site will teach people to create a budget and stick to this protecting their family from hardship. This site pretends to help to understand debt and show a way to get out of debt. Managing financial crises and investing for the future is a hard goal to achieve, but this is a continuous to blessing for us and others. This web site will be based on Personal Finances for Self-Reliance Published by The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. What if we are in financial crises? I could be great to know how to get out of debt and know a way to manage the financial crises.

Audience

The main audience for this project is people between their 15's and 60's, people on his first job, people knowing nothing about their future and people enjoying their retirement. This site looks for those that want to learn and/or improve their personal finances, and people looking for God' assistance to get out of their own financial gaps. It is suitable if our main audience think God can bless their live if they are compliant to guidance of the Holy Spirit.

Money to assist the needy cannot come from an empty purse. Support and understanding cannot come from the emotionally starved. Teaching cannot come from the unlearned. The Basics of Finances

- Learning to be grateful and becoming wise:
“I urge you . . . to look to the condition of your finances. I urge you to be modest in your expenditures; discipline yourselves in your purchases to avoid debt to the extent possible. Pay off debt as quickly as you can, and free yourselves from bondage. ” (“To the Boys and to the Men,” Ensign, Nov. 1998, 54)
- Understanding Budgets
"A budget is a plan. Using a budget, you plan how you will use your money for a certain period of time. Following a budget will help you and your family take control of your temporal life… Budgeting can also help you to protect your family from hardship as you use it to allocate money for building an emergency fund, paying down debt, and saving for future expenses. We cannot predict the future, and costs often arise unexpectedly. It is important to remember that a budget must be flexible—continually adjusted and improved. When you create a budget… remember that it will need to be consistently reviewed and revised. If you go over budget, don’t give up! It may take several months of adjustments before you have an effective budget."
- Understanding Debt
"Debt is borrowing money that is not yours. It typically comes with a cost, known as interest. Interest is a percentage of the amount owed. You end up paying more for what you borrowed, sometimes a lot more. Debts come with expected payments, and an overall expectation to repay all that you borrowed plus interest."
- Learning to Invest
"When people hear the term investing, they may think of a loud and chaotic trading floor with people selling stocks and bonds. While that may be part of investing, investing is also the act of putting time, effort, or money into something and expecting some type of a return. In this sense, investing is an element of self-reliance. You will explore three ways to invest: 1. Save money 2. Pursue home ownership 3. Seek education"
- Teach others, practice gratitude
"When we become temporally self-reliant, it is our duty to help others also become temporally self-reliant. One of the best ways to help others become self-reliant is serving and giving to others." "A unified approach to financial stewardship can ultimately bring gratitude, harmony, and peace

Budgeting Rule

The Essentials, Wants and Savings Budgeting Rule or The EWS Budgeting Rule is based on the popularized 50/30/20 Budget.
However, the 50/30/20 Budget can be unfeasible for people with low incomes living in areas with a high cost of living. For example, housing, utilities and health care costs would increase over 50% of their income.
The EWS Budgeting rule as the 50/30/20 Budget refers to dividing up your after-tax income (earnings) among essentials, wants, and savings; but being more flexible, you can adjust the EWS percentage according to your income and necessities. A balanced budget can help you determine how much you should be spending, and on what.
Essentials are things you certainly have to pay, or at least you cannot live without very easily, these include things like rent, groceries, transportation, and utilities.
Wants are stuffs you desire but do not need that all to survive. These include vacations, dinning out, streaming services, and hot drinks.
Savings include retiment contributions and debt payment.
How it works. Know your monthly income, know how much you receive in your bank account. Know how much you spend, classify your expenses by categories and check up where you are over spending. Plan your budget avoiding debt and over spending. Fanally, follow your budget.

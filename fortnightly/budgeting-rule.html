Self-reliance Basic of Finances Budgeting Rules

The Essentials, Wants and Savings Budgeting Rule or The EWS Budgeting Rule is based on the popularized 50/30/20 Budget. However, the 50/30/20 Budget can be unfeasible for people with low incomes living in areas with a high cost of living. For example, housing, utilities and health care costs would increase over 50% of their income.

The EWS Budgeting rule

The 50/30/20 Budget refers to dividing up your after-tax income (earnings) among essentials, wants, and savings; but being more flexible, you can adjust the EWS percentage according to your income and necessities. A balanced budget can help you determine how much you should be spending, and on what.

Essentials

Essentials are things you certainly have to pay, or at least you cannot live without very easily, these include things like rent, groceries, transportation, and utilities

Wants

Wants are stuffs you desire but do not need that all to survive. These include vacations, dinning out, streaming services, and hot drinks.

Savings

Savings include retiment contributions and debt payment. How it works. Know your monthly income, know how much you receive in your bank account. Know how much you spend, classify your expenses by categories and check up where you are over spending. Plan your budget avoiding debt and over spending. Fanally, follow your budget.
